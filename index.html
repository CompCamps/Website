MSI CompCamps

Annual Summer Camps

Every year, CompCamps provides one-week camps for students aged 12-17. The camps provide a fun environment to explore technology with a group of like-minded peers. Campers are able to pick one or more areas to focus on for a project that they will demo at the end of the week.

What Will Campers Learn?

Campers will learn the basics of logic, programming, website design, game development, and electronics. With a mix of instructed and open project time, the campers are able to explore these areas in their own way.

Guest Speakers

Every year, we invite guest speakers to talk about their experiences in the tech industry. These speakers provide insight into the various career paths available in technology and inspire campers to pursue their interests.

Who Should Attend the Camp?

CompCamps is designed for students aged 12-17 with an interest in technology and its role in our world. Campers should have a basic understanding of computers and be ready to learn more about programming and electronics.

Where Do the Camps Take Place?

Our camps are held in partnership with the University of Regina's Computer Science Department. The camps take place in the university's computer labs, giving campers access to the latest technology and resources.

Who Runs the Camp?

Mentor Systems Inc. is a member-based organization registered as a non-profit corporation in Saskatchewan, Canada. MSI was formed by volunteer camp staff who were counsellors and mentors at the various annual computer camps. Since the camps started in 1991, MSI has had the mandate of providing technological opportunities for youth.
